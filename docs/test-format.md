# Hunter Admission Test Format Guide

## Hunter College High School (HCHS) Entrance Exam

### Overview

The HCHS Entrance Exam is administered in January each year to eligible 6th graders. As of January 2026, the exam contains both Hunter-developed content and licensed content from the Independent School Entrance Exam (ISEE) owned by the Educational Records Bureau (ERB).

### Eligibility Requirements

- Must reside in New York City
- Must meet qualifying scores on 5th grade NYS English Language Arts (ELA) and Mathematics Tests
- Currently enrolled in 6th grade

### Exam Structure

The exam consists of two test booklets with four sections total:

#### English Booklet (110 minutes total)

| Section | Format | Time (suggested) |
|---------|--------|------------------|
| Reading Comprehension | 50 multiple-choice questions | 70 minutes |
| Writing Assignment | Essay or creative piece (up to 2 pages) | 40 minutes |

#### Math Booklet (75 minutes total)

| Section | Format | Time (suggested) |
|---------|--------|------------------|
| Quantitative Reasoning | 37 multiple-choice questions | 35 minutes |
| Mathematics Achievement | 47 multiple-choice questions | 40 minutes |

### Section Details

#### Reading Comprehension

- 5-6 passages of varying lengths
- Every fifth line is numbered for reference
- Passage types include:
  - Non-fiction excerpts
  - Poetry (multiple stanzas)
  - Fictional dialogue
  - Novel excerpts
  - First-person memoir pieces
- Question types:
  - Vocabulary in context
  - Main idea and theme
  - Author's purpose and tone
  - Inference and interpretation
  - Text structure and organization

#### Writing Assignment

- Essay or short creative piece (up to two pages)
- Prompts often involve topics about New York or growing up
- Some years offer a choice of topics; other years have only one
- Scored on:
  - Originality
  - Effectiveness
  - Use of detail
  - Grammar and mechanics

#### Quantitative Reasoning

- Tests mathematical reasoning abilities
- Question types:
  - Word problems
  - Quantitative comparisons
  - Pattern recognition
  - Logical deduction
- Focus on reasoning rather than procedural recall

#### Mathematics Achievement

Tests middle school curriculum-based skills including:
- Computation with fractions, decimals, percents, and integers
- Estimation
- Rules of divisibility
- Simple probability
- Rate, average, and ratio
- Time and money calculations
- Area and perimeter
- Visual and numerical pattern recognition
- Three-dimensional figures
- Multi-step word problems

### Scoring Process

1. Multiple-choice sections are computer-scored and hand-checked
2. A cut-off score is established based on overall applicant performance
3. Only students meeting the cut-off have their essays evaluated
4. Essays are read anonymously using identification numbers
5. Students with stronger essays are offered admission

### Important Rules

- **No calculators allowed**
- **No guessing penalty** - answer every question
- Use the test booklet for scratch work
- Do not remove any pages from the booklet
- All answers must be recorded in the answer booklet

### Admission Statistics

- Only 6-8% of test-takers gain admission
- Approximately 200 students are admitted each year
- The cut-off score varies annually and is not published

## Hunter College Elementary School (HCES)

### Overview

HCES admits students for Kindergarten entry through a two-round assessment process.

### Important Notice

**HCES strongly discourages test preparation.** The school states that teaching these tests in advance invalidates results and reserves the right to disqualify candidates showing prior test exposure.

### Round 1 Testing

**Format:** One-on-one assessment with an HCES-approved psychologist

**Duration:** 30-60 minutes

**Cost:** $375.00 (financial assistance available)

**Language:** English only

**Tests Used (may include):**
- Stanford-Binet Intelligence Scales (5th Edition)
- Woodcock-Johnson Tests of Cognitive Abilities
- Wechsler Intelligence Scale for Children (WISC)
- Kaufman Assessment Battery for Children

### Round 2

- No fee charged
- Occurs in mid-January
- Requires observation forms from parents and preschool teachers

### Key Points

- Parents cannot be present during testing
- Scores are not shared with parents or third parties
- Children must demonstrate adequate English proficiency
- No official sample questions are provided (by design)
