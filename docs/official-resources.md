### Official Sample Materials

The official HCHS website provides:
- Two sample entrance exams (not actual past exams)
- Tips and strategies for each section
- Test day information
- Frequently asked questions

**Note**: These sample tests illustrate question types but are not actual administered exams. Sample Exam #1 contains fewer Reading Comprehension questions than the actual test.

### 2026 Exam Changes

As of January 2026, the HCHS Entrance Exam includes:
- Hunter-developed content (Reading Comprehension and Writing Assignment)
- Licensed content from the ISEE (Independent School Entrance Exam) for Quantitative Reasoning and Mathematics Achievement sections

### Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible to take the HCHS Entrance Exam, students must:
1. Reside in New York City
2. Be currently enrolled in 6th grade
3. Meet qualifying scores on 5th grade NYS English Language Arts (ELA) and Mathematics Tests

- **Admission Procedures**: [hunterschools.org/elementary-school/admissions/procedures](https://www.hunterschools.org/elementary-school/admissions/procedures)

### Important Notice About Test Preparation

**HCES strongly discourages test preparation.**

From the official website:
> "HCCS strongly discourages exposing children to any test in preparation for the Hunter admissions process. 'Teaching' these tests in advance invalidates the outcome."

The school reserves the right to disqualify any child if there is evidence of prior exposure to testing materials.

### Tests Used for HCES Admission

Round 1 testing may include standardized cognitive assessments such as:
- Stanford-Binet Intelligence Scales (5th Edition)
- Woodcock-Johnson Tests of Cognitive Abilities
- Wechsler Intelligence Scale for Children (WISC)
- Kaufman Assessment Battery for Children

These are proprietary psychological instruments, and official sample questions are not provided.
