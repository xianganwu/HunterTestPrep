# Sample Essay Response - Prompt 2: The Sound of Home

## Prompt

Every place has its own sounds—the rumble of the subway, the hum of the refrigerator, voices calling from down the hall. These sounds become so familiar that we often stop noticing them until they're gone.

**Write about the sounds of a place that feels like home to you.**

---

## Sample Essay (High-Scoring Response)

### The Kitchen Symphony

Every Saturday morning begins the same way in our apartment: with the sound of my father's slippers shuffling across the kitchen floor at 6:15 AM. I am not awake, not really, but somewhere between sleep and waking I register this sound—that distinctive slide-step rhythm that means the weekend has begun.

What follows is a symphony I could conduct with my eyes closed. The cabinets opening with their soft wooden complaint. The clink of ceramic as he selects his favorite mug, the one with the chipped handle that my brother made in second grade. The rush of water into the kettle, followed by its slow-building whistle that rises like a question until someone removes it from the flame.

Our kitchen is small—barely room for two people to stand without bumping elbows—but its sounds fill our entire home. By 7:00, my mother is awake, and I hear her singing along to the radio in a voice that never quite finds the right key but somehow makes every song better. The refrigerator hums its steady bass note underneath everything, a sound so constant that I only notice it when the power goes out and the sudden silence feels like a missing heartbeat.

The sounds layer and build. My sister's bedroom door creaking open. The bathroom faucet running through its mysterious sequence of groans and gurgles that only our building knows. The neighbors upstairs beginning their weekend routine, their footsteps crossing our ceiling like distant thunder.

What I love most is the conversation that emerges from these sounds. My father asks about my homework while the eggs sizzle in the pan. My mother reminds my sister about her soccer practice while cabinets open and close around her words. We are all talking over each other, interrupting, laughing, and somehow understanding everything that matters.

Last summer, I spent two weeks at my aunt's house in a quiet suburb. Her kitchen was three times the size of ours, with modern appliances that barely made a whisper. The house sat alone on its lot, surrounded by green lawn, and at night the silence was so complete I could hear my own breathing.

I couldn't sleep.

It took me several days to realize what was wrong. I missed the sounds of my home—the rumble of the subway two blocks away, the late-night conversations drifting up from the street, the endless percussion of my family's movement. I had never thought of these sounds as music before, but in their absence, I understood that they had been the soundtrack of my entire life.

Now, when I wake on Saturday mornings to the shuffle of my father's slippers, I listen more carefully. I hear not just sounds, but stories—of routine and ritual, of a family living their lives in a small space, of ordinary moments that add up to something extraordinary. Home, I've learned, is not just a place. It's a song that plays whether or not you're listening, always there, always welcoming you back.

## Scoring Analysis

### Strengths of This Essay

**1. Evocative Opening**
The essay begins with a specific, sensory image (father's slippers shuffling) that immediately draws the reader in.

**2. Rich Sensory Detail**
- "soft wooden complaint" of cabinets
- "clink of ceramic"
- "slow-building whistle that rises like a question"
- "like distant thunder" for neighbors' footsteps

**3. Extended Metaphor**
The essay develops the metaphor of a "symphony" throughout, tying sounds to music and creating cohesion.

**4. Narrative Structure**
The essay includes a mini-story (the visit to aunt's house) that provides contrast and deepens the central insight.

**5. Reflection and Insight**
The writer doesn't just describe sounds—they explain what these sounds mean and how understanding changed through experience.

**6. Strong Conclusion**
The final paragraph returns to the opening image (Saturday morning) while expanding its meaning.

## What Makes This Essay Stand Out

1. **Specificity**: The writer doesn't just say "kitchen sounds" but describes exact moments: the 6:15 AM slippers, the chipped mug from second grade, the faucet's "sequence of groans."

2. **Contrast**: The visit to the quiet suburban house provides dramatic contrast that deepens the essay's meaning.

3. **Reflection**: The writer moves beyond description to understanding, showing growth in perception.

4. **Sensory Integration**: Multiple senses are engaged—sound is primary, but we also feel the small kitchen, see the ceramic mug.

5. **Emotional Resonance**: The essay creates genuine feeling without being sentimental, finding meaning in everyday sounds.

---

## Tips from This Example

1. **Choose a specific place**: The writer focused on one kitchen, not "home" in general.

2. **Use timing**: The Saturday morning structure gives the essay natural organization.

3. **Include contrast**: The aunt's quiet house shows what the sounds mean by showing their absence.

4. **Layer details**: Start with one sound, then add more, building a complete picture.

5. **End with insight**: Don't just describe—show what you learned or realized.

## Sample Essay Response - Prompt 6: A Difficult Decision

*(Shorter example to show variety)*

### The Tryout

I had practiced for three months. Every afternoon, I ran drills in the park until the sun went down and my legs burned. The basketball tryout was on Friday, and I was ready.

But then Ms. Chen posted the dates for the spring musical auditions. Same day. Same time.

I had been in every school musical since fourth grade. Theater was where I belonged—where I knew every cue, every blocking mark, every way to make my voice fill a room. Basketball was new, a challenge I had set for myself, a way to prove I could do something that didn't come naturally.

My mother said to follow my heart. My best friend said I was crazy to give up what I was good at. Neither of them understood that the decision wasn't about basketball or theater. It was about who I wanted to become.

I chose basketball.

I didn't make the team.

But something unexpected happened in the weeks that followed. I kept practicing—not for a spot on the roster, but because I had discovered something important: I loved the process of trying, even when success wasn't guaranteed. Theater had always felt safe, my talent a cushion against failure. Basketball had no such cushion, and that vulnerability felt more honest.

This year, I auditioned for the musical and tried out for basketball again. I was cast in the chorus and made the JV team. Neither was a starring role, but both felt earned in ways I hadn't experienced before.

The difficult decision taught me that the goal isn't always to choose correctly. Sometimes the goal is simply to choose deliberately—to understand why you're making the choice you're making, and to learn from wherever that choice leads you.

---

### Scoring Note

This shorter essay (approximately 300 words) demonstrates:
- Clear structure (situation, decision, outcome, reflection)
- Specific details without excessive length
- Honest reflection on failure
- Mature insight in conclusion

**Estimated Score: 32/36 (Very Good)**

The essay could be improved with more sensory detail and a more developed scene, but it effectively addresses the prompt and demonstrates thoughtful reflection.
